Wheelchair-Savvy: Innovating the Structure of Wheelchairs

May 2017 - May 2021
Chung Yuan Christian University
Biomedical

Abstract

As population aging accelerates, Taiwan faces a growing elderly care challenge, with 3.5 million seniors and only 300,000 caregivers. Traditional wheelchairs often lead to chronic health issues like hunchback and poor circulation due to prolonged sitting. To address this, we are developing a self-supporting wheelchair that allows users to switch between sitting and kneeling positions, reducing discomfort and promoting mobility. By integrating electromyography (EMG) analysis, we aim to optimize posture transitions for maximum comfort and stability. This innovation enhances independence for the elderly and disabled while alleviating caregiver shortages.

The What

With Taiwan expected to enter a "super-aged society" by 2025, an increasing number of elderly and disabled individuals require wheelchairs for mobility. However, prolonged sitting in traditional wheelchairs leads to pressure ulcers, spinal issues, and poor blood circulation. This study aims to explore whether posture-adjustable wheelchairs can alleviate these issues by distributing pressure more effectively and enhancing user comfort.

The How

We designed a posture-adjustable wheelchair that allows users to switch between sitting and kneeling positions. Using the BPMS pressure distribution measurement system, we analyzed the effects of these postural changes on peak pressure in key areas such as the ischial tuberosity, greater trochanter, and sacral region. We categorized pressure zones into low, medium, and high levels and compared their distribution between sitting and kneeling postures.

Results & Impact

The results showed that the kneeling posture significantly reduced pressure in the ischial tuberosity and greater trochanter regions (p < 0.001), while pressure in the sacral region slightly increased due to weight redistribution. The total contact area also decreased, but low-pressure areas increased, suggesting better pressure distribution. This indicates that a posture-adjustable wheelchair can help prevent pressure ulcers, improve comfort, and reduce caregiver burden, making it a valuable innovation for the aging population.
