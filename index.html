About Me

Biological Data Science Graduate Student

I am a graduate student in Biological Data Science at ASU with a background in biomedical engineering and a strong focus on applying data-driven solutions to complex healthcare and research problems. My expertise includes machine learning, biosignal processing, clinical data analysis, and end-to-end system development.

During my recent summer internship, I developed an end-to-end AI safety detection system to improve LLM moderation reliability, combining custom-trained models with full-stack deployment. Currently, my master’s thesis focuses on drug–drug interaction prediction using LLMs and graph generative networks (GGNs), aiming to enhance pharmaceutical safety through deep learning and biomedical knowledge graphs.

My interdisciplinary background allows me to bridge the gap between biomedical challenges and AI-driven solutions. Whether through system development or clinical data analysis, I strive to deliver impactful, scalable technologies that improve real-world outcomes.
